Asking Good Questions

12/06/2014

back to blog index

While I rely on the Internet for finding the answers to many (perhaps too many) questions these days. I find that I am rarely the primary inquirer on forums. That said, I have always been able to find the information that I need, and when I need a more in depth explanation, I usually find a personal contact. I know I need to get into practice of asking questions online, but this system has worked so well thus far and saved me the scorn of trolls.

Alas, regardless of the forum, the key to a good question (or set of questions) is clarity and brevity. People are eager to tell you what they know but you have to make it easy/fun for them. Also, they will usually come into a situation thinking they know what you want to know, and when that doesn't exactly align with what you're seeking, that's when clear questions can get you back on track.

I think the reality, is that having a larger question (thesis, if you will) is important, but more often than not you can divine the answer that larger question by getting the answers to several smaller simpler questions. Furthermore, often you start off and you think you know what you're trying to find out, but in reality it is not until you ask a lot of questions that you realize what your real question is. Sometimes asking the wrong question is the key to understanding what the right question.
